Concept demo for Kenneth — a "find your physio" front door + SEO pages Read the idea ▾

The problem today. Each Physiolab location runs a separate Jane account, so a new patient has to pick a clinic before they know who works there or whether anyone there treats their problem — then they're dropped into a generic booking list. Nearly every physio clinic in the city has this exact same front door.

The idea. Let people start from their need — "I want help with…" — and filter by location, language and funding, then deep-link straight onto the right practitioner's Jane page. (The "Book with…" buttons below are live deep links into your real Olympic Village booking — try Kenneth's.)

Why it's a competitive advantage:

  • Differentiation — you'd own "find the right physio for me," not "find a clinic near me." I haven't found a local competitor doing need-first discovery.
  • Better first visits & retention — patients land with someone who actually treats their issue (vestibular → Judy, pelvic → Mecca, running → Kelly).
  • Fewer mis-bookings & admin — the funding filter stops someone booking an ICBC visit with a private-pay-only clinician.
  • SEO you're currently missing — condition/service pages (ICBC, pinched nerve, IMS, decompression) catch high-intent Google searches and funnel into the finder.
  • Cheap to adopt — it's a thin layer over your existing Jane accounts; the deep links already work, so your web provider can rebuild this inside the real site.

A working prototype to react to, not a finished product — your web provider can rebuild it inside the real site. Source code & notes: github.com/svongripp/physiolab-demo (honest caveats there — tags are drafts; only Olympic Village is populated).
