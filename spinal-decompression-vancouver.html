Spinal decompression therapy in Vancouver

Spinal decompression is a gentle, non-surgical traction technique that eases pressure on spinal discs and nerve roots — an option some people find helpful for disc-related neck and back pain, and one most clinics in the city don't offer.

The idea behind it

By applying a controlled, intermittent stretch to the spine, decompression aims to slightly reduce pressure within the disc and open up the space around an irritated nerve root. The goal is to take load off the structures that are reproducing your pain so that movement and exercise become more tolerable.

Where the evidence sits, honestly. Decompression isn't a magic fix, and the research is mixed. We treat it as one tool inside an active rehabilitation plan — useful for the right presentation, alongside manual therapy and exercise, not a stand-alone cure. We'll tell you plainly whether we think it's likely to help in your case.

Who it may suit

  • Disc bulge or herniation with nerve-related leg or arm symptoms
  • Pinched-nerve pain that's sensitive to compression and loading
  • People looking for non-surgical options to pair with an exercise program

Who it isn't for

Decompression isn't appropriate for everyone — for example, where there's fracture, significant osteoporosis, certain spinal hardware, or some other conditions. That's why it always starts with a proper physiotherapy assessment rather than booking the machine sight-unseen.

Does it hurt?
It's usually a slow, comfortable stretch. You stay clothed and lie on a table while the traction cycles on and off.
How many sessions?
It's typically a short course paired with exercise, reassessed as you go rather than sold as a fixed package.
Is it covered by insurance?
When delivered within a physiotherapy visit it's generally covered like other physio treatment; we'll confirm for your plan.
